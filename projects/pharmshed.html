Research Contributor · Machine Learning & Data Engineering

PharmShed is an interdisciplinary research project predicting pharmaceutical concentrations in municipal wastewater from population demographics. The goal is to model how the drug consumption profile of a community — derived from census data — maps to the chemical signature of its sewage, enabling cost-effective environmental monitoring without direct sampling.

  • Built a synthetic population generator for Clark County, Nevada using US Census demographic data, applying stratified sampling by age, sex, and county to produce statistically representative drug-use profiles.
  • Developed multi-label classification models (ML-kNN and One-vs-Rest SVM) trained on 217 pharmaceutical compounds, mapping population features to predicted wastewater concentrations.
  • Conducted a quality-control audit of an external validation dataset containing 2,647 pharmaceutical measurements, identifying and flagging unit inconsistencies and systematic data entry errors.
  • Built a 200-drug synonym dictionary using regex text mining and an OCR pipeline (pdfplumber + tesseract) to reconcile drug name variants across clinical and environmental datasets.
  • Tools: Python, pandas, scikit-learn, pdfplumber, tesseract, ML-kNN, One-vs-Rest SVM, regex text mining.
